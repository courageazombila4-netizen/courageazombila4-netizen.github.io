Bachelor of Sustainable Mining Program

Overview

This programme trains students to manage mineral extraction with minimal environmental impact, social responsibility, and economic sustainability.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand mine lifecycle planning and rehabilitation.
  • Apply environmental management techniques and monitoring.
  • Assess socio-economic impacts and stakeholder engagement.

Core Modules

  1. Mineral Economics
  2. Mine Design & Planning
  3. Environmental Management
  4. Occupational Health & Safety

Duration & Entry

Typically 3-4 years full-time. Entry: secondary school certificate with relevant science subjects.

Assessment

Combination of assignments, field reports, practical labs, and final exams.

Resources

  • Lecture notes and field guides
  • Case studies on mine rehabilitation
  • Data sets for practical exercises

Practice Questions

  1. Explain three strategies for reducing environmental impact of mining.
  2. Outline the steps in planning a small-scale mine rehabilitation.

Geology Subject

Overview

Geology studies Earth materials, processes and history. It provides the foundation for mineral exploration, environmental assessment and geotechnical engineering.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify common minerals and rock types.
  • Interpret geological maps and cross-sections.
  • Explain geological processes such as volcanism and sedimentation.

Modules

  1. Mineralogy
  2. Petrology
  3. Structural Geology
  4. Field Mapping

Assessment

Lab work, field reports, map exercises and written exams.

Practice Questions

  1. List and describe the three main rock types.
  2. Draw a simple rock cycle and explain each transition.

Chemistry Subject

Overview

Covers fundamental principles of matter, reactions and laboratory practice essential for many science and engineering fields.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand atomic structure and the periodic table.
  • Perform basic quantitative calculations and lab techniques.
  • Predict reaction behavior and balances.

Modules

  1. General Chemistry
  2. Organic Chemistry Basics
  3. Analytical Techniques

Assessment

Laboratory reports, quizzes and theory exams.

Practice Questions

  1. Define matter and list its common states.
  2. Describe the structure of an atom.
